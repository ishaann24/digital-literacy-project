Poor digital communication can lead to serious misunderstandings, especially in professional settings. For example, a team working on a group project relied only on short text messages to assign tasks. One member misunderstood a vague instruction and completed the wrong section, causing delays and confusion just before the deadline. Since there was no clear clarification or confirmation, the mistake went unnoticed until it was too late, affecting the overall quality of the project. This problem could have been avoided by using clearer communication methods, such as detailed emails, shared documents, or brief video meetings. Confirming instructions and encouraging questions would have ensured everyone understood their roles correctly. Effective digital communication requires clarity, proper tools, and timely feedback to prevent such issues.
